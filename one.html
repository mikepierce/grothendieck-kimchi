Kimchi is a preparation of fermented vegetables, by a "lactic" fermentation like sauerkraut, in the traditional Korean way. Kimchi is, with rice, the base of Korean food, where a meal (even a light one) without one or the other is unthinkable. This is equally true during the 6 months of the year in Korea where fresh vegetables are available, and accompany the meal jointly with Kimchi.
